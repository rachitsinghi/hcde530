C4 — APIs and Data Acquisition
Working with APIs shifted my understanding of data from something static to something that is actively structured, queried, and shaped through decisions. Instead of just “getting data,” I had to read documentation, identify the right endpoint, and understand how the API’s response schema translates into something usable. For example, when calling the Pexels Search API, I wasn’t just retrieving images, I was parsing a nested JSON response to extract specific fields like alt text, avg_color, and image URLs, and then deciding how to structure that into a CSV for downstream analysis . This forced me to think about data not as given, but as something that needs to be filtered, interpreted, and reshaped depending on the use case.

What became especially clear is that APIs don’t give you exactly what you need, they give you raw material. The real learning came from what I chose to do with that data. For instance, I extended the pipeline by downloading images from the returned URLs and then analyzing them for pill shape and color, combining text-based parsing with image-based heuristics when metadata was incomplete . This made me confront ambiguity in real-world data and design fallback strategies, which is very different from working with clean datasets. I also learned the importance of responsible API usage, managing API keys through environment variables instead of hardcoding them ensured my workflow was both secure and reproducible . Overall, this assignment moved me from “using data” to actually thinking like someone who acquires, evaluates, and operationalizes it.
